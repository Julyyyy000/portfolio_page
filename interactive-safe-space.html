Overview

Role: self-developed — programmer / crafting / artist

Safe Space is a projection-mapping interactive installation exploring mental illness, fragile emotional environments, and the tension between comfort and instability. A physical room corner is transformed using handmade props and generative, reactive projections.

Medium & Tools

  • Projection mapping onto a crafted physical environment
  • TouchDesigner for generative animation & interactivity
  • Hand-built room corner, props, miniature environmental objects
  • Video and still documentation

Concept & Experience

The installation visualizes how memories shaped by mental illness blur comfort and threat. Projection layers flicker and drift across the space, echoing the emotional instability of trying to create safety while dealing with intrusive thoughts.

Safe Space rewards slow observation—tiny animated details and atmospheric responses appear only when the viewer lingers, mirroring the internal negotiation of vulnerability and comfort.
